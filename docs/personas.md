# Personas

## The Patient (or guardian)

A person — often a mother registering a child — who wants their (or their
child's) critical facts available in an emergency without carrying paperwork
or repeating their history to every new clinician. Primary need: control
over what's shared, and confidence it will be there when needed.

## The Health Worker (Attester)

A licensed, registered clinician or nurse who can vouch for a record's
accuracy. Primary need: a fast, low-friction way to attest without needing
crypto expertise — the Soroban interaction should be invisible to them.

## The Community Health Worker (CHW)

Often the same kind of person as the attester, but framed around outreach:
goes into communities to register patients who would otherwise never get a
card. Primary need: a sustainable reason to keep doing this work — the USDC
incentive.

## The Responder

An emergency room clinician, ambulance staff, or first responder who has
never met the patient before. Primary need: trustworthy facts in seconds,
with zero setup — no login, no app install, just a scan.

## The Funder

A grant program (e.g. Stellar Community Fund) or DPG-aligned funder
evaluating whether Lafiya is real infrastructure or a demo. Primary need:
evidence that Stellar is load-bearing (not decorative) and that funds map to
measurable, on-chain-traceable outcomes.
